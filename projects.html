Projects

Below are selected ongoing and recent projects that reflect my current interests in theoretical security, mathematical modeling, and robust machine learning.

High-Dimensional Cryptographic Transformations

A conceptual research direction investigating whether low-dimensional plaintext can be embedded into high-dimensional structures, transformed in that space, and projected back into outputs that are harder to reconstruct or interpret directly.

Game-Theoretic Secret Sharing

A formal modeling project on secrecy maintenance in distributed environments where participants face different incentives, disclosure costs, and strategic pressures. The goal is to understand protocol stability under rational behavior.

Noise-Robust Traffic Sign Recognition

Study of denoiser-classifier pipelines and end-to-end classifiers for image recognition under Gaussian noise, with experiments on GTSRB using CNN and ResNet-18 architectures.
