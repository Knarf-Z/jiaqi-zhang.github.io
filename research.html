Research

My research interests currently center on cryptography, formal security reasoning, game-theoretic modeling, and robust machine learning systems under noisy or adversarial conditions.

Road Sign Classification with Denoising Pipeline Approach
Co-authored research paper | Computer vision, denoising, classification, robustness

This work studies traffic sign classification under noisy conditions and compares two approaches: end-to-end classification versus a denoiser-classifier pipeline.

The project evaluates performance on the German Traffic Sign Recognition Benchmark (GTSRB), with Gaussian noise injected at multiple levels, and compares CNN and ResNet-18 models. The central question is whether a pre-trained denoising stage can improve classification robustness under noisy conditions.

The results suggest that the denoising pipeline can help CNN models under moderate noise, while ResNet-18 generally benefits less and often performs better in an end-to-end setting.

Current Direction 1

Game-Theoretic Secret Sharing. I am interested in formalizing incentive and leakage trade-offs in distributed secrecy settings, especially when agents may act strategically rather than honestly.

Current Direction 2

High-Dimensional Cryptographic Transformations. I am exploring abstract ways to transform low-dimensional plaintext into richer high-dimensional structures before re-projecting into forms that are harder to reverse engineer.
